India's Health and Well-Being

by: Sameer Komoravolu

Cultural Aspects:

  • Sankranti- This is a harvest festival where farmers offer newly harvested grains to the community and to animals, and promotes the well-being of the community.

  • Diwali- This is the festival of lights. It indicates prosperity for the community.

  • Ugadi- This is the festival of spring: new mango blossoms are made into a dish and consumed. This is intended to promote health.

Facts About Health and Well-Being:

  1. India has a life expectancy of 68.56 years
  2. As of 1960, India had a life expectancy of 41.17 years

Sources include: World Bank

Food in different regions of India

  • In North India, the climate is cooler, so wheat is the primary crop, and bread is the staple food.
  • In South India, the climate is more tropical, so rice is grown consumed more often.
  • In coastal regions, seafood is commonly eaten.
